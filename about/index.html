About

Hello! I’m a second-year psychology PhD student at the University of New South Wales. My research interests lie in understanding the way that uncertainty influences people’s decisions, particularly those involving risk and long-term consequences; for example, how we make decisions around saving for retirement or fighting against climate change.

While completing my PhD, I am currently working at the Commonwealth Bank of Australia as a behavioural scientist in their Financial Wellbeing team. My role involves a combination of research and application, aiming to take an evidence-based approach to improving Australians’ financial outcomes.

My current aspirations are to continue improving as a research-practitioner – someone who can bring innovative ideas and rigour from research, as well as practical experience, to solve challenging societal problems.

If you would be interested in connecting, feel free to reach out via email or Linkedin!
